Climbing

Climbing is considered an extreme sport by many, but climbing can also be broken down into several subcatagories with varying degrees of extremeties.

These would include:

  • Bouldering
  • Sport
  • Trad
  • Speed

Bouldering is what I spend most of my attention on. It involves shorter climbs that require strength and technique to get through.

Sports climbing is about climbing high walls while attached to a rope (unless you're Alex Honnald). This style of climbing does not require as much strength as bouldering but is very enduring.

Trad climbing is by far the most extreme. Where as in sports you will have anchor points drilled into the wall for you to clip your rope into incase you fall, trad climbing requires you to carry up cams and nuts that you will place into cracks in the wall for you to attach your rope to.

Speed climbing is only seen in a competitive environment, such as in competitions and the Olympics. Speed climbing will always involve the same route and is all about getting to the top of the wall in the fastest time possible.

When climbing with a rope, as you would normally do in sport or trad climbing, you will wear a harness. A harness is a piece of equipment that you wear over your pants and is strapped tightly to your legs and waist. The climbing rope is then tied to the harness once it is securely tightened. The purpose of harness is to catch you if you fall while climbing on a high wall, and safely distribute force to your waist. Climbers use the word "wipper" when someone has taken a big/bad fall.
